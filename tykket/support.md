## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I add an app to track?**
Tap the + button in the top-right of the sidebar and enter your app's name and choose an icon.
You can add as many apps as you like.

**How do I create a ticket manually?**
Select an app in the sidebar, then tap the + button in the toolbar. Fill in the user's email,
subject, and message body and tap Add.

**How do I connect my support email inbox?**
Go to Settings → Email Account and enter your IMAP server details (host, port, username, and
password). Most support email providers — including Gmail with an app password — work with
standard IMAP over SSL on port 993.

**Emails aren't appearing in my inbox — what should I do?**
Check your IMAP credentials in Settings → Email Account. Make sure IMAP is enabled in your
email provider's settings (for Gmail, enable IMAP in Gmail Settings → Forwarding and POP/IMAP).
If you're using Gmail, use an App Password rather than your main account password.

**How do I reply to a ticket?**
Open a ticket, then tap the Reply button in the toolbar. Type your reply and tap Send. Tykket
sends the reply directly from your device via your connected IMAP account, threaded to the
original email.

**How do I close a ticket?**
Open the ticket and tap the status badge at the top of the detail view. Select Closed from
the picker.

**How do I delete a ticket or app?**
To delete a ticket: swipe left on the ticket row in the list and tap Delete.
To delete an app: long-press or right-click the app in the sidebar and tap Delete App. This
also deletes all tickets associated with that app.

**Is my data backed up?**
By default, data is stored on-device only. Enable iCloud Sync in Settings to sync tickets and
projects across your devices via iCloud.

**How do I restore a tip purchase?**
Tips are consumable purchases and cannot be restored — this is standard App Store behaviour
for consumable IAPs. If you have a question about a tip charge, contact Apple Support at
reportaproblem.apple.com.

**Is my IMAP password secure?**
Yes. Your IMAP credentials are stored exclusively in the iOS Keychain using Apple's Security
framework. They are never stored in the app's database and never transmitted to Mosswell Studios.

**I have a question not listed here.**
Email us at support@mosswellstudios.com and we'll get back to you as soon as possible.
